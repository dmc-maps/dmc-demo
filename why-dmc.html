Why Digital Mapping Consultants

What matters to engineering firms evaluating a drone subcontractor.

Sole-Operator Consistency

Every project is led by Devin Clark: FAA Part 107 certified, MS Geography (GIS and Remote Sensing), with field experience at MassGIS and years of drone data collection across civil engineering, structural inspection, railroad corridor mapping, and environmental documentation work. There are no subcontractors on DMC projects. The person who answers your calls before the project is the person flying on the day of capture and delivering the package when it's done. Engineering firms that have worked with larger drone vendors know the variability that comes from rotating field operators. DMC doesn't have that problem by design.

Engineering Subcontract Experience

DMC's current client base includes civil and railroad engineering firms in New England, specifically firms that need GeoTIFF basemaps for AutoCAD underlays, corridor maps with environmental zone overlays, parking utilization studies for campus planning, and survey-grade topographic mapping with GCP-controlled accuracy. The facade inspection documentation pipeline described here was developed for a structural engineering firm holding NYC FISP inspection contracts, on a live ten-face building. DMC understands what engineering firms need from a data collection subcontractor: deliverables in formats they can use immediately, communication at a professional level, and no surprises on scope or quality.

Code-Generated Deliverables

The interactive HTML viewer you can open from this site is not an off-the-shelf product. It was built in Python and HTML/CSS/JavaScript specifically for this inspection workflow: loading engineering drawings as grid references, pairing RGB and thermal imagery by zone, and providing annotation tools that work offline without any subscription or platform dependency. The export pipeline (Python script, JSON draft format, zip output) was built for an active subcontract relationship and iterated through real project delivery. This capability, building custom tooling that makes the delivered data immediately useful rather than handing off a folder of images, is DMC's primary differentiation from drone operators who fly and deliver raw footage. There is also a technical reason for this approach: facades are flat, repetitive, and reflective, which causes photogrammetry and 3D stitching to fail on them. A drawing-indexed record is not a downgrade from a 3D model, it is the format that actually works on these buildings. If your firm has a recurring inspection or mapping workflow that would benefit from a structured deliverable format, that's a conversation worth having.
